Featured Repertoire

A deeply moving meditation on mortality and remembrance, composed for viola and strings in a single evening.

Viola Concerto in G Major, K. 216

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

A transcendent interpretation of Mozart's elegant concerto, showcasing the viola's lyrical capabilities in this masterful adaptation.

Viola Sonata Op. 120 No. 1

Johannes Brahms

Brahms' late masterpiece captures profound emotion and technical brilliance, exploring the full expressive range of the viola.

A symphonic journey featuring the viola as protagonist, telling the story of a wandering poet through the Italian countryside.

Lachrymae Op. 48a

Reflections on a song of Dowland, weaving Renaissance melancholy through modern harmonies with exceptional artistry.

Sonata for Viola Alone

A tour de force of contemporary composition, pushing the boundaries of solo viola technique and expression.

Anton Boutkov has established himself as one of the foremost violists of his generation, captivating audiences with his profound musicality and technical excellence.

A graduate of the NYU School of music, Anton has performed with major orchestras including the Springfield Orchestra and the Boston Symphony Orchestra.

His interpretations are praised for their emotional depth and scholarly insight, bringing fresh perspectives to both classical masterworks and contemporary compositions. Anton's commitment to expanding the viola repertoire has led to numerous collaborations with talented musicians and composers.

When not performing, Anton serves on the faculty at the UMass school of Music and maintains an deep underground recording collection that he will publish one day.
